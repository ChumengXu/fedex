Comedy Central, long thought of as a cultural institution, is reeling from the power of the Internet: How can it continue to dominate TV if no one watches TV? Jonah Weiner profiles Kent Alderman, the president of content and original programming, as part of an investigation of how the network is trying to stay fresh. It turns out that one of the network's biggest fears - online viewership - may be what saves it.
Alexis Okeowo profiles Thulisle Madonsela, South Africa's first female public protector, who has been given the job of fighting corruption at the highest levels of the government. Emily Bazelon writes about whether judges, often thought to be the stabilizers of society, are allowed to change their minds. Bazelon cites, among others, her grandfather, David L. Bazelon, who reversed one of the biggest decisions of his career. Taffy Brodesser-Akner chats with George Takei on his "feud" with William Shatner, Sasha Frere-Jones recommends his favorite Fort Greene bodega and Francis Lam learns how to make tortilla española.
