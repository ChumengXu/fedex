"Our demand is simple," Johnetta Elzie, one of the social-media activists behind the #blacklivesmatter movement, tells Jay Caspian Kang in the cover story of this week's magazine. "Stop killing us." Kang followed Elzie and another activist, DeRay Mckesson, for several months from one police-brutality demonstration to the next, from Ferguson, Mo. to New York to Baltimore on the eve of the city's riots late last month. The result is a probing portrait of the new civil rights movement that has pushed the issue of policing and race into the center of our national consciousness.
The photographer Giles Price, too, found himself in the thick of the news last month while on assignment for us in Nepal, where we sent him to shoot the scene at the Mount Everest base camp. When a 7.8-magnitude earthquake struck, Price took his camera to Kathmandu and photographed the aftermath. He brings us a haunting before-and-after portrait of a country in its moment of crisis.
