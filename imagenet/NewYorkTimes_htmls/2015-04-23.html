The image you see on the cover of this week's special Walking New York issue of the magazine isn't Photoshopped - it's real. It's by the French artist JR, who is known for pasting giant photographs on urban surfaces all over the world. Earlier this month, JR photographed 16 recent immigrants to New York for the magazine. One of them was Elmar Aliyev, a 20-year-old waiter who immigrated from Azerbaijan last August.
JR and his team printed Aliyev's picture on 62 strips of paper, then took them to the Flatiron plaza at 4 a.m. on a Saturday morning. The result was a 150-foot-tall image of Aliyev, which was then photographed from a helicopter. There are probably easier ways to make a magazine cover, but for our New York issue, we wanted to do something as wildly energetic as the city itself. Be sure to watch our stunning time-lapse video of the whole endeavor.
As for the other 15 images, JR's team is posting them around all five boroughs of the city tonight - and if you live around here, we hope you'll go look for them this weekend. (The weather's supposed to be great.) Follow us on Twitter for updates as the project unfolds.
If you'd rather stay home, we hope you'll at least venture online and help us out with another Walking New York project. We asked an array of New Yorkers - novelists, musicians, mail carriers, can collectors - to tell us about their most memorable walks in the city, and we've plotted the results on an interactive atlas: a map of love letters to a beguiling, confounding city. For the next couple weeks, we will be gathering more memories of walking New York from our readers and will be including them in the project; do tell us about your own.
Also in this week's issue: Christopher Griffith's arresting photos of shoe shiners' hands (be sure to look at this one on your smartphone for the full multimedia experience); Susan Dominus's dispatch from the front lines of New York's eternal battle between pedestrians and cars; Ryan Bradley's exploration of how rats navigate the city (spoiler: they like the subway); new work from Christoph Niemann, Edwidge Danticat, Lee Friedlander, Teju Cole, Nathaniel Rich and Ben Schott; and much much more.
Happy reading,
