"The perplexing but wonderful thing about Toni Morrison's career," Rachel Kaadzi Ghansah writes in her cover story on the Nobel Prize-winning novelist from this week's Times Magazine, "is just how much her prominence was created not by the mainstream publishing world, but by Morrison herself, on her own terms, in spite of it." Ghansah digs deep into Morrison's art and assesses the state of black literature in general - and asks why Morrison's own vision for it seems further away than ever.
Also in print and online this week, Sam Anderson pens a lovely appreciation of "the dysfunctional not-quite-romance" on "Veep" between Vice President Selina Meyer (Julia Louis-Dreyfus) and her loyal bag man, Gary Walsh (Tony Hale); Emily Bazelon makes the case for compensating the victims of wrongful convictions; Mark Leibovich asks whether calling our politicians "polarizing" says more about them or ourselves; and Tamar Adler celebrates the culinary potential of the lowly sesame seed.
Happy reading,
Jake Silverstein
