In this week's magazine, we look at the future of money - specifically, the ways in which technology is upending how we spend, save and even define currency. Gideon Lewis-Kraus brings us the saga of ZPM Espresso, whose founders learned the hard way what happens when the expectations of Kickstarter backers collide with the thorny realities of manufacturing. Nathaniel Popper writes to us from Argentina, where a volatile currency and a chaotic financial sector have created the perfect opportunity for Bitcoin brokers. And Malia Wollan considers a very different kind of marketplace: an attempt to build a better system for organ donation based on technology and altruism.
Also in print and online this week, Michael Pollan dissects the idea of "natural" food, Jonathan M. Katz explains how not to cover an earthquake, Francis Lam explores Manhattan's Curry Row and Troy Patterson considers the upscaling of distressed jeans.
