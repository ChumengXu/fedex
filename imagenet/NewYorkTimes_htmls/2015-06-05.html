Welcome to the age of digital imperialism. Your guidebook is our Tech & Design issue, in which we explore how Silicon Valley has shaped the world beyond America's borders, and what the world thinks about it.
In Russia, Adrian Chen uncovers a pro-Kremlin "troll" farm, an office in which employees are paid to disseminate false information and vitriolic opinion on the Internet - and finds himself a target of one of their attacks. Clive Thompson ponders what the rise of the Apple smartwatch means for the Swiss watchmakers who dominate the high-end of the industry with their analog craftsmanship. Jenna Wortham compares the "chaotic, multifunctional, exciting" app marketplace in South Korea with the United States, and finds plenty for Palo Alto to worry about. And Liz Alderman brings us inside the Parisian police campaign against UberPOP, an arm of the car-service startup that allows any licensed driver to operate as a cabbie.
There's plenty more - Gideon Lewis-Kraus on computer translation, a photo essay on digital-downloading markets in Mali, a collection of short essays on the way that email has shaped our lives - but I'll log off and let you get to exploring on your own.
Happy Reading,
Jake Silverstein
