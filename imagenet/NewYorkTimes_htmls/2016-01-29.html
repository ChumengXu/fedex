What do parrots know about PTSD? Turns out, more than we thought. In this week's cover story, Charles Siebert writes about Serenity Park, a small sanctuary in California where damaged parrots, abandoned by their owners, and traumatized veterans, suffering from post-traumatic stress disorder, commune to provide care and therapy for each other. "When I learned of a sanctuary in which parrots and war veterans are healing one another, I knew I had to write about it," Siebert told us. "I became so addicted to the parrots – one night I went back after closing hours just to peer into their enclosures. It's the nearest we can get to time travel, staring into a parrot's eyes."
Elsewhere in the magazine, Matthew Shaer pieces together the story of the deadly wreck of Amtrak train 188, which derailed and crashed just northwest of the 30th Street Station in Philadelphia last May. What caused the worst American rail disaster in decades? Robert Draper profiles Senator Ted Cruz and his campaign's attempts to win the votes of evangelicals, Amanda Hess parses the suffix "-phobic" in social discourse, Jia Tolentino wrestles with her complex feelings about the South at Cracker Barrel and Ana Marie Cox interviews the conservative radio show host Hugh Hewitt.
