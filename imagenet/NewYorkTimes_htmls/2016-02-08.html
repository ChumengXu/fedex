Your Friday Briefing
By ADEEL HASSAN
Good morning.
Here’s what you need to know:
• Slugging it out.
Bernie Sanders and Hillary Clinton clashed over the role of money in politics, one of the searing exchanges in a debate on Thursday night in New Hampshire over which Democrat was the most progressive.
Mrs. Clinton was painted as Wall Street’s friend and defender. Here are highlights from the night and fact checks of the candidates’ statements. Young Democrats, who are flocking to Mr. Sanders, are making the race more competitive.
On the Republican side, the Jeb Bush and Chris Christie campaigns are escalating their attacks on Marco Rubio, trying to stem his rise in the polls.
• The migrants’ plight.
As European Union foreign ministers begin a two-day meeting in Amsterdam to discuss peace efforts in Syria and Libya, thousands of Iraqis who joined the great migrant wave to Europe are coming home disappointed.
In Syria, government forces are advancing on Aleppo, once the nation’s largest commercial center. Its loss would be a major blow to the rebels.
More than $10 billion was pledged on Thursday to help millions of suffering Syrians, but there is no end in sight to the war, which is about to start its sixth year.
• State Department emails.
A dozen emails containing classified information were sent to the personal email accounts of Colin L. Powell and close aides of Condoleezza Rice during their terms as secretaries of state.
The emails have been classified as “confidential” or “secret” as part of a review process that has resulted in similar upgrades of information sent through the personal email server that Hillary Clinton used as secretary of state.
• Support for WikiLeaks founder.
A U.N. panel said today that the WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange was being detained arbitrarily in the Ecuadorean Embassy in London and should be released and compensated.
Britain said today that it would arrest him if he tried to leave.
• Hearing in “Serial” case.
A hearing for Adnan Syed, convicted of the murder of his former high school girlfriend, concludes today in a Maryland court.
A judge is expected to announce sometime after it ends whether he will grant Mr. Syed, who was the initial subject of the popular “Serial” podcast, a new trial.
BUSINESS
• The Labor Department will release figures this morning on hiring and unemployment in January.
Economists will be closely watching the report to see how the job market has been holding up, given the drop on Wall Street since the beginning of the year.
• The threat posed by an overhang of bad loans appears to be rising, with China as the biggest source of worry.
Some analysts estimate that China’s troubled credit could exceed $5 trillion, equivalent to half the size of the country’s annual economic output.
• China is spending billions of dollars on a push to make its own microchips. That could strengthen its military capabilities, which worries Washington.
• Facebook announced that “each person in the world” is separated from every other by “an average of three and a half other people.”

BACK STORY
Imagine you’re a coach in the N.F.L. championship game. You’re getting ready for the big game after finishing short in the title contest a year before.
Then one of your team’s biggest stars calls to tell you he can’t make it.
That’s what happened to Vince Lombardi, the head coach of the Green Bay Packers, in 1961.
In response to the construction of the Berlin Wall, thousands of military reservists and National Guardsmen had been activated.
Among the dozens of players that received call-up notices in October was Paul Hornung, who left the Packers midseason to report to Fort Riley in Kansas. He was to use his weekend passes to join the team for games.
The championship game was scheduled for New Year’s Eve in Green Bay. But Hornung’s Christmas leave ended before then.
So Lombardi, who had met President Kennedy at a dinner and had been given the number of his private line, called the White House. Kennedy, a football fan, called Fort Riley and Hornung’s furlough was changed.
Hornung, who was both a rusher and kicker, scored 19 points, as Green Bay humiliated the New York Giants, 37-0.
It was the first of Lombardi’s five championships plus two Super Bowl victories. The Vince Lombardi trophy will be awarded to the Super Bowl 50 winner on Sunday.
